WHAT IS ALOPECIA AREATA?

Alopecia areata is a common type of hair loss that affects many people in India. This autoimmune disorder causes hair to fall out in small patches, leaving bald spots on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, face, and other parts of the body. The condition occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks the hair follicles, resulting in hair loss.

WHAT CAUSES ALOPECIA AREATA?

01

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder in which the immune system attacks the hair follicles, resulting in hair loss. The exact cause of this condition is still not fully understood, but it is believed to be a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

02

Alopecia areata may be triggered by different factors, such as stress, infections, medications, and hormonal changes.

03

Family history of autoimmune diseases or with other autoimmune disorders may have a higher risk of developing alopecia areata.

04

Some genetic variations may also be associated with an increased risk of alopecia areata, according to research by the American Academy of Dermatology.

05

However, having these genetic variations does not necessarily mean that a person will develop alopecia areata.

06

Despite ongoing research, the exact cause of alopecia areata is still unknown, and further studies are needed to fully understand the underlying mechanisms of the condition.

Androgenic Alopecia

It is the most common type of alopecia in men, also known as male pattern baldness. It also affects women and is commonly called female pattern baldness. The cause can vary from family history of hair loss to hormonal issues in women.

Alopecia Areata

Characterized by hair loss in patches on the scalp or on the body, Alopecia Areata is the result of an autoimmune disorder in which the body’s own defence system attacks its own cells.

Alopecia Totalis

Alopecia Totalis refers to complete loss of hair on the scalp. When not treated in early stages, alopecia areata can progress to alopecia totalis.

Alopecia Universalis

This type of hair loss is characterized by complete loss of hair on the body. It is linked to alopecia areata where the immune system fights not just the scalp hair, but body hair as well.

Traction Alopecia

It occurs due to pulling force being applied to the hair. They are mainly found in women. But if not corrected soon, it may lead to permanent hair loss.
